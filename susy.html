Supersymmetry (AKA, "Susy") is a hypothetical extension to the Standard Model, which is currently not proven by experiment, but nonetheless, remains highly popular among many theoretical physicists. In a model that is supersymmetric, the equations governing matter, and the equations governing force, are the same. The key prediction of supersymmetry is that every particle in the Standard Model should have a more massive relative (a "superpartner", or "sparticle"), who's spin differs by 1/2. Supersymmetry is popular because it seems to have the potential to answer some big questions that the Standard Model does not describe, such as: providing a compelling candidate for dark matter, explaining the mass of the Higgs Boson, and much more.
