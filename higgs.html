The Higgs Boson, or simply "the Higgs", is the wierdest of the Standard Model particles/fields. While most quantum fields have a value of almost zero in a vacuum (quantum fluctuations notwithstanding), the Higgs field takes on a possitive value everywhere, including in otherwise empty space. The role of the Higgs field is to give mass to elementary particles, because without it, conventional quantum field theory predicts that all particles should be massless, which simply cannot be correct.

The Higgs has a mass of 125.09 GeV, neutral charge, and zero spin.
